Privacy Policy

Effective: 2026-04-30 · Last updated: 2026-04-30 · Contact: hamzahussainshaah@gmail.com

This Privacy Policy describes how the TouchifyMouse mobile app handles information when you use it. The app turns your phone into a wireless trackpad, keyboard, microphone, and remote speaker for a desktop computer running the TouchifyMouse desktop companion on the same local Wi-Fi network.

1. Summary

  • Trackpad input, keystrokes, audio, and pairing data flow directly between your phone and your own desktop computer over your local Wi-Fi network. None of it touches our servers.
  • Sign-in is optional. You can use the entire app anonymously. If you choose to sign in with Google, we store a small profile and device record in Firebase (see Section 3).
  • We do not sell, share, or transfer your personal information to third parties for their independent use.

2. Permissions and how we use them

Camera (android.permission.CAMERA)

Used only to scan the pairing QR code shown by the desktop companion app. Camera frames are processed on-device by the Google ML Kit barcode scanner embedded in the app and are not stored, recorded, transmitted, or sent anywhere. The camera is only active while the QR scan sheet is open.

Microphone (android.permission.RECORD_AUDIO)

Used only when you explicitly enable the "Use phone as microphone" feature. Audio captured from your phone's microphone is streamed in real time over your local Wi-Fi to the paired desktop computer that you own. Audio is not recorded, persisted, or sent to any third-party server. The microphone is silent until you toggle the feature on, and stops immediately when you toggle it off or disconnect.

Network

(INTERNET, ACCESS_WIFI_STATE, ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE, CHANGE_WIFI_MULTICAST_STATE) Used to discover and connect to the desktop companion on your local Wi-Fi via mDNS (Bonjour) and to maintain the TCP/UDP connection that carries trackpad, keyboard, media, and audio data. The internet permission is also required by Google AdMob and Google Play Billing.

3. Account sign-in and Firebase

Sign-in with Google is optional. The app is fully usable without an account. If you choose to sign in, the following is stored in Google Firebase (services we use as a data processor):

Firebase Authentication

When you sign in with Google, Firebase Authentication receives your Google account email, display name, profile photo URL, and a unique user ID assigned by Firebase. Firebase handles credential exchange — we never see or store your Google password.

Cloud Firestore — what we store under your account

If you are signed in, the app writes the following to a private document in Firestore keyed to your Firebase user ID:

  • Profile: email, display name, profile photo URL, sign-in provider, account creation timestamp, last-seen timestamp.
  • Devices: for each phone or tablet you sign in on — platform (Android/iOS), OS version, device model and manufacturer, app version, build number, a vendor-supplied non-resettable device identifier (Android ANDROID_ID or iOS identifierForVendor), and first/last seen timestamps. This is used to help you recognise which device is which in future "your devices" features.
  • Connection log: when your phone successfully pairs with your desktop, we record the desktop's name, operating system, local IP address, port, and the connection timestamp. This is used to power features like "recent connections" on signed-in accounts.

Read access to this data is restricted by Firebase Security Rules so that only your own signed-in account can read your own data. We do not have a public dashboard exposing this information.

Firebase Crashlytics

Crashlytics records anonymous crash reports (stack traces, OS version, device model, app version) to help us fix bugs. Reports are not linked to your email or sign-in identity. Crashlytics is disabled in debug builds.

Firebase Analytics

We collect basic, anonymous usage events (e.g. "app opened", "feature used") to understand which parts of the app people use. Analytics events are not linked to your email or sign-in identity.

How to delete your Firebase data

To delete your sign-in profile, device records, and connection log: email hamzahussainshaah@gmail.com from the Google account you signed in with, and we will delete your users/{your-uid} document and all subcollections within 30 days. You can also revoke the app's access to your Google account at https://myaccount.google.com/permissions.

4. Third-party services

Google AdMob

The app displays advertisements through Google AdMob. AdMob may collect and process information such as advertising ID, IP address, coarse location (derived from IP), device type, and basic interaction events for ad delivery, frequency capping, and fraud prevention. Google's handling of this data is governed by Google's own privacy policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy.

You can reset your Android Advertising ID, opt out of personalised ads, or delete it at any time from your Android device settings under Settings → Privacy → Ads.

Google Play Billing (in-app purchases)

If you make an in-app purchase, the transaction is handled by Google Play. We receive a confirmation token from Google to unlock the purchased feature, but we do not receive or store your payment-card details. Google's handling of payment data is governed by Google's privacy policy.

Google Play Services and ML Kit

The app uses Google Play Services and Google ML Kit (for QR decoding). These are governed by Google's privacy policy.

5. Data we do NOT collect

  • Your phone number or postal address.
  • Your Google password (Firebase handles authentication; we never see it).
  • Photos, videos, contacts, calendar, files, or SMS.
  • Precise GPS location.
  • Health, biometric, or financial information.
  • The content of your trackpad gestures, keystrokes, microphone audio, or media commands — these flow only between your phone and your own desktop on your local Wi-Fi.

6. Data on your device

The app stores small, non-sensitive preferences locally on your device using Android's standard SharedPreferences (e.g. selected theme, pointer sensitivity, last connected device). This data never leaves your phone and is removed when you uninstall the app.

7. Children's privacy

The app is not directed to children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information, contact us and we will take steps to address it.

8. Security

Connections to your desktop companion happen over your own local Wi-Fi network. We recommend you use a trusted, password-protected Wi-Fi network. We do not use TLS for the local connection because the data never leaves the LAN; please ensure your home/office Wi-Fi is properly secured. Sign-in tokens and Firestore traffic to Firebase are encrypted in transit (HTTPS) by the Firebase SDK.

9. Your rights

If you have signed in with Google, you can request access to, export, or deletion of your stored Firebase data (profile, device records, connection log) by emailing hamzahussainshaah@gmail.com from the Google account you signed in with. We will respond within 30 days.

To control AdMob and Google Play data, use your Android device settings and your Google account controls (https://myaccount.google.com).

To delete all app data on your device, go to Settings → Apps → TouchifyMouse → Storage → Clear Data, or simply uninstall the app.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be indicated by a revised "Last updated" date at the top. Continued use of the app after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
